Enter the exciting world of Louis Wain, the iconic British artist who was born in 1860s London, only to find himself at the centre of the Great British puplic's attention thanks to his now-iconic and unique artwork, featutring anthropomorphised cats and kittens.

Featured Art: 'The Cat Who Got the Cream'

As with a lot of Louis Wain's artwork, this one is typical of what I have seen over the years. Wain's use of non-standered colours on the two cats is as delightful as it is strange. The carpet that this feline family are laying on is betaful, and shows how he had a keen eye for detail, even if the end result is rather kitch.

While we don't know what he was thinking when this was made, I, for one, think that the cats has a very curius look to them, like they just spotted something very strange indeed - prehaps they saw a pink dog.
