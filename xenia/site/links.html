Books

Reading is good for you!

Because Internet: Why does the Internet use certan terms and phrasies? Learn that and so much more in Gretchen McCulloch's Because Internet: Understanding the New Rules of Language.

Crucial Interventions: A horrible book that shows the golden age of surgery in loads of full-paged illustrations and paintings. Lots of fun.

Knowledge is Beautiful: For any nerds who love facts and satistics, this is for you. I spent hours just taking in all of this infomation in this book.

Masters of Doom: The 2004 classic of how John Romero and John Carmack created one of the best computer games of all time: Doom.

Weird Medieval Guys: How to Live, Laugh, Love (and Die) in Dark Times: Shocker. I liked this book full of weird and wonderful medieval art.

Games

I may be a 40-odd-year-old fox, but I love gaming! So here is a fun selection of free games that you can play now :0

LibreQuake: I loved Quake growing up in the 1990s, yeah, Doom was fun, but come on! It's Quake! I've talked to the person who is the head of this project a few times, that being Miss Lavender, seems to all-in-all be very nice.

SuperTuxKart: Banger. Such a fun racing game that I love to play whenever I have friends around and also play online. Also what's really cool is that someone made a kart of me >:D

Freedoom: I loved Doom growing up in the 1990s, yeah, Quake was fun, but come on! It's Doom! I've talked to the person who is the head of this project a few times, that being Simon Howard, seems to all-in-all be very nice.

Minetest: It's crazy to think that there are people making whole games 100% for free and are as good as the legit things. Minetest is a great example of this (also try MineClone2!).

SuperTux: Amazing. I love this game so much and so should you!!

The Simulator: I still remmeber when one of my school friends sent me this URL in 1998 (over IRC!!). It's a fun little game that's oddly funny at times.

Tools

Here are a number of things that I use on the daily that I think you'd find very useful.

Kiwix: So as I live in the middle of no where, a lot of the time my internet can be very slow. So bascally over a good few days, I left my computer on 24/7 while I downloaded a torrent of Wikipedia - and guess what? I can use Kiwix to view that website (as well as loads of other wikis) even without internet! So, so useful.

Hitchhiking on Wikivoyage: A very, very useful guilde on how to,.. well, hitchhike across the place.

DuckDuckGo: This might be one of the best search engines that I know of. I stopped using Google in 2016, so this was a good replacement.

Castles of Wales: Info for castles in the UK... well, Wales...

Mastodon: Social media is a plague upon the World Wide Web - Mastodon, not so much.

I Know What You Download: Hey kids, did you know that you should be safe when downloading anything online?

Great Firewall of China Firewall Test: I must say that I love the government of China and her censorship of the WWW.
