By Xenia, 2023-XX-XX

Let's cast your mind back all the way to around 1220 or 1260, oh man, what a time to be alive, such fun times. During this period lived a boy in Novgorod (now Veliky Novgorod, Russia) called Onfim. Sadly, very little is known about this child as a whole, but what we do know can be seen as a look in the mind of a Middle Ages child.

Hi, I'm Xenia and I've been looking at art by Onfim as of late and thought that I should make something that was inspired by him, so I wanted to make this little blag post about how much I love his art and the history behind it.

During the 1200s in Russia, paper certainly did exist, *however*, it was costly to use, so the average person didn't make use of it, instead, they would opt to use bitch bark. Simply put, you would go in a forest, cut the top layer of skin off of the tree, boil it, and boom, you have something that you can write on by using your handy knife. These birch barks really weren't uncommon at all at the time, and in fact, Novgorod would even have a bit of deforestation in the area due to how popular this method would become.
