History of The Cult

 The Chandler family wanted to put their past behind them, so as an effort, moved to Whitewood, a small island with a population of just over 100 people at any given time. The family would soon become quite well-known on this island, even getting to the point where they managed to gain the most land to crop.

Harry would soon start his education at The St. Dio Church of England School, where he learnt the story of God, and his only son, Jesus Christ. Harry would grow fond of the many stories in The Bible, he especially enjoyed reading and learning about the many bloody parts in the Holy Book. Harry would write in his diary about how much the idea of war excited him. Over the year of 1999, a number of documents made by the police were leaked, among the reports were of his diary, written when he was around 16.

His diary contained a large number of references to that of religious texts, including what could be considered to be the start of what would become The Fellowship of The Dead Owl.

As Harry got in his late teens, he started to develop an unnormal obsession with animals, most noticeably he had an interest in birds, especially in owls. Harry would sometimes go out in the middle of the night with an air rifle, and shoot birds. He would then take them home, and conduct his own sick, and twisted experiments on the animals. Around this time his mother would develop tetanus, and needed t be taken back to mainland Britain to be treated, as the island only had a very basic supply of drugs.

In 1982, Harry's mother's health got much worse, soon his father decided that it was best that he should visit his wife, as she was very near Death's door.

Harry was left on his one to look after the crops, and farm animals. Not much is known about late 1982 in the Chandler family, only that Harry's father, John, lived in mainland England with his wife.

On the 2nd of January 1983, Helen Saxon lost her life. It is unclear when she was born, some people suspect that she was in her early 50, but even that is debated. Helen's final resting place would be at sea.

This event would lead to Harry going deeper down the rabbit hole that was his insanity. It is known that Harry would start to take a large number of psilocybe mushrooms (better known as "magic mushrooms"), that grew on the land of Whitewood. It was thanks to this that he was able to come up with his many strange ideas, for example, he believed that "Christ" "told" him that he should start up a new religion.

What is quite amazing is that he was able to indeed start up a new form of Christianity, and was even able to gain a large number of followers.

The religion was officially called "New Christian", however, this name didn't really last long, as people in the cult would start to call it "The Fellowship of The Dead Owl", Harry himself would adopt this name. The religion believed in the majority of The Bible, however, the cult would take more of a Satanice root, so much so that they would conduct a large numbers of black masses. No photos or videos of these events were taped, however, they were recorded by non-cult members in the form of text-logs.

According to events written down, the members of the cult would set up a large fire, hold hands, and run around the fire, all while whering potato-like bags over their head, to potect who they were. While this was going on, Harry Chandler would sit down, and quote lines from The Bible.
