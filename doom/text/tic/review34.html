 Review date: 18. May 95                             Review No.:  34
---------------------------------------------------------------------
    Title: Blue Hell                Game: Doom 2
 Filename: MKBLUHEL.ZIP             Size: 105k

   Author: Mark Anthony Klem      e-mail: mark.klem@swcbbs.com

     Maps: MAP01                    Type: normal/deathmatch level
   Sounds: yes, weapons
 Graphics: yes, walls
    Music: yes

[ Level construction ] ----------------------------------------------

  The whole level is symmetrically constructed, made for deathmatch,
I think.  The author used a few  special-tricks,  sometimes your are
wondering where you are, it's amazing!  Rooms are  changing from one
moment to another,  monsters are  attacking you and  you just wonder
what happend. Great thing.  Sadly the level is kinda small, but this
is better for deathmatch games though.

[ Texture design ] --------------------------------------------------

  Well designed, the whole level is in blue colors (the author added
some blue extra walls). The labyrinth looks awesome.

[ Bugs ] ------------------------------------------------------------

  No HOMs, Medusa effects or Tutti-Frutties. No bugs at all.

[ Enemies ] ---------------------------------------------------------

  Like I'm  playing every  level in UV,  I tried  it too... but it's
much too hard I think.  Right after starting  you will meet a Cyber-
demon, but don't think this  is going to  be easy (risky prediction,
isn't it?), because behind the  next door is an Arch-Vile waiting to
blow you!  Then I played  it in Ultra Easy  and it was not very easy
though. I think the whole level suffers from this.
  But, I have to say that after playing the level once you will know
where to get  the weapons  and it  might be  possible  to kill those
bastards even in Ultra Violence.

[ Things ] ----------------------------------------------------------

  Well placed, a lot of ammo, health and other stuff.

[ Scoring ] ---------------------------------------------------------

  88%

  Worth downloading, particularly to check out the special effects.
