Did you know?

This WAD was made by Milo Casali, who along with his brother, Dario, would make Final Doom a year later in 1996, an officially licensed Doom game.

As well as making this WAD, both of the Casali brothers also worked on a number of maps for Memento Mori, a collaborative project that was, in part, made by The Innocent Crew - the same people who wrote these reviews.

 Review date: 20. May 95                             Review No.:  38
---------------------------------------------------------------------
    Title: Butcher                  Game: Doom 2
 Filename: BUTCHER.ZIP              Size: 48k

   Author: Milo Casali            e-mail:
                                     D.Casali@Rea0808.wins.icl.co.uk

     Maps: MAP01                    Type: normal level
   Sounds: no
 Graphics: no
    Music: no

[ Level construction ] ----------------------------------------------

  Wow. This is what I call a nice construction. Sure, this one could
be one of  id's levels.  Great light usage  and key placement.  What
should I say, you have to play this one in any case.

[ Texture design ] --------------------------------------------------

  The level is one of these  'stone & wooden' levels.  Looks awsome.
Clean work. I noticed only one misaligned texture.

[ Bugs ] ------------------------------------------------------------

  No HOMs, Medusa effects or Tutti-Frutties. What did you expect?

[ Enemies ] ---------------------------------------------------------

  Uuhhh... not easy though.  But it's possible  to kill them without
cheating (of course),  especially the end 'room' with its teleporter
is VERY hard. But just take a look behind you. ;) However, very well
placed enemies (oh, those Revenants, argh!).

[ Things ] ----------------------------------------------------------

  Perfect amount of ammo and health. Enough other things. Great.

[ Scoring ] ---------------------------------------------------------

  94%

  Worth downloading; UV only for advanced players. :)
